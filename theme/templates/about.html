About

My name is Eric Kim - Web Developer, Data Scientist, and Petroleum Engineer.

I'm currently a senior undergraduate student at the Univeristy of Texas at Austin, Hildebrand Department of Petroleum Engineering, the #1 petroleum engineering school in the US. I am a self-taught Python developer with strong engineering & statistical background. I'm most familiar with Python, but lately I've been involved more with full-stack web development powered by Django-backend, that includes PostgreSQL database, linux-server configuration, and user interface design for Oil & Gas industry.

Right now my primary focus is on building my set of skills in statistics (especially geostatistics). I eventually want to move on to developing sophisticated algorithms that involve machine learning, but before I do that, I wanted to have strong background knowledge in statistics.

The posts in this blog will mainly be about statistics, not machine learning. There are already more than enough of tutorials for Python machine learning, but there aren't that many learning materials that talks about Python application for statistical problems. I feel like statistics are neglected on the internet due to the recent boom in artificial intelligence, but in fact there are so many things you can do with basic statistics that does not involve any machine learning.

I hope my blog posts help for those people who want to have strong basis in statistics
